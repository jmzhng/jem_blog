A Need for Humanity

Jul 19, 2026

The idea of a permanent underclass is an idea that gets thrown around the tech world. The idea that a system of 'techno-feudalism' will dominate society, in which the elite 0.1% leverage AI agents to do all of their work, eliminating the need for humans to power society. To fight against this, many people in the SF tech world go gung-ho, working ridiculous 100-hour weeks grinding startups or tech jobs, with the hope of making it rich and buying assets so they can live free for the rest of their lives.

With how fast AI-technology and how the culture has been progressing throughout the years, the idea morphs from a far-fetched conspiracy theory to a looming reality. Every single tech CEO is talking about how AI is going to transform life as we know it, championing ideas like 'people don't need to work anymore!'. The culture corroborates what they are saying, companies such as Meta have been experiencing mass layoffs, there are new startups harnessing AI to streamline every single conceivable job done by a human, and it seems harder than ever for regular people to find even basic jobs.

Every single AI-spokesperson tries to market these ideas in both a positive and negative light. People like Jensen Huang proudly declare that AI will eliminate large sums of the workforce and that people should be ready. Meanwhile, people like Dario Amodei try to paint AI as a force for good, allowing for society to be more productive and build great things. Neither of these viewpoints inspire confidence in the ordinary man. Jensen seems ready to lay-off his entire workforce at a moments' notice, while Dario seems to have good intentions, every single increase in productivity (such as the industrial revolution) has been used to simply increase profits for shareholders, while workers are bound to de-skilled and stagnant jobs.

It's a tale as old of time, the people at the top use every single opportunity to stamp on the people below them for their own personal gain. The rich control the government and the masses, with their multi-million dollar donations to super PACs and their control of the mainstream news and social media. Every single successful CEO with enough capital to strongly influence culture either straight up lies about their commitment to society, or their vision is blurred by the mountains of money they sit behind. Capitalism isn't a zero-sum game, it's just a game where rich people win every single time. Or, if you want to move ahead, you have to sell a bit of your soul.

Wealth inequality has gotten to a point in which 1. basic needs by the masses (food, shelter, security) are starting to erode, and 2. economic power has been completely monopolized by the rich (and in a capitalistic society, power in general) in which the common man can no longer accumulate wealth.

When I step outside, while it's not a liberal nor conservative hellscape, I see signs that society is decaying. Roads and buildings are neglected at the side of the road, people who walk with grimaces and limps, people who look overworked and sad, cars with plastic bags instead of windows, and homeless people who society seems to treat as nothing more than a bag of trash lying on the side of the road. At college, many of my peers constantly talk about the fear of not being able to find jobs. Job fairs are crammed with people, yet no one seems to ever seem to find anything of note. I usually just grit my teeth whenever someone from HR talks to me about their company 'culture', while politely tossing my resume into the trash.

People are starting to become desperate or depressed. Desperate people are afraid, they spread hateful rhetoric about immigrants and culture war. Their frustration turns into anger and fear, and thanks to the 2nd Amendment, that fear can turn deadly. These incidents are all the current news cycle and social media serve up, feeding our minds that our neighbor is a threat to our own safety. People become depressed, unable to cope with society. They numb their pain with digital distractions, afraid to confront the world at their doorstep. They've quietly given up on humanity, choosing to live the rest of their lives in a tomb.

All the while the rich consolidate their wealth, fueled by a upper-middle class that is fueled by the fear that they'll become 21st century peasants.

This regression in society fills me with a sickening feeling of dread about the future of this country. I recognize that the world is not all that bad as I make it out to be right now, but the current trajectory is frightening. Sometimes it motivates me to grind more LeetCode questions so maybe someday I can laugh in a hot-tub atop a New York City penthouse with Peter Thiel. Other times, it turns me into a depressive, anxious wreck who no longer believes in the good of humanity.

The need for humanity has not been lost. I still hold onto the seed of hope that the good of humanity will prevail in a time in which it is unattractive. People are what make life worth it. Help yours.
